Vegas game plan

Saturday is the flex night. Sunday is the committed Vegas day: use the MGM daybed as home base, keep the boys in and out of the lazy river, then stack one memorable late-afternoon experience with one memorable night show.

Weather feel

  • Vegas: hot pool weather — save most outdoor walking for after sunset.
  • Zion: warm enough for short sleeves, still worth an early shuttle start.
  • Bryce: cold mornings and evenings — layers matter.
  • Valley of Fire: comfortable if you hit it before mid-day heat builds.

Money saver note

For this exact route, paying individual entrance fees at Zion and Bryce is slightly cheaper than buying the America the Beautiful pass.

Sunday, March 22

MGM daybed all day, then F1 and a family show

This is the most efficient Vegas day of the trip: use the daybed as your basecamp, let the boys drift in and out of the pool, then finish with one split-plan activity and one family show.

Sunday anchor

Do not over-plan the first half of the day. The daybed is the reason Sunday works so well. Stay in the lazy-river rhythm for as long as it feels good, then transition into the evening plan.

Tesla cheat sheet

  • Vegas: top up before Monday departure
  • Vegas → Zion: Mesquite or St. George if you want buffer
  • Zion/Bryce leg: leave Springdale strong
  • Cedar City: easiest Bryce-area safety valve
  • Henderson: clean charge before final home leg

Simple packing emphasis

Shorts and pool gear for Vegas, hiking clothes and lots of water for Zion, and real warm layers for Bryce sunrise and sunset. Keep jackets and long pants easy to reach, not buried.
